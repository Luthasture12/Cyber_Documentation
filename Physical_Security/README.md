# Physical Security

Protection of assets from threats, such as theft or damage

Three areas of focus
    Prevention
    Detection
    Recovery


What to protect:
    Workstations
    Servers
    MDFS, IDFs - 
    Documents - physical paper
    Backups
    Cables
    Connection Points

Ways to prevent
    Doors (DMZ)
    Manage/close switches, ports
    Log out of workstation
    Personnel
    HVAC
    Cameras
    Walls/Cages for server racks
    Physical Access Control
    Fences

    SIMPLE WAYS
        Put computers out of reach (just monitor on desk that customers see, for instance)
        Taping lines on floor
        Lock up equipment
        Lights
        Shredding paper
        Destroying backups
        PErform regular checks/walkthroughs
        Teaching employees

    NONE OF THIS IS FOOLPROOF, ALWAYS WAYS TO CIRCUMVENT. OUR GOAL IS NOT TO FULLY PREVENT (WHICH IS IMPOSSIBLE), BUT TO MITIGATE THE RISK AS MUCH AS REASONABLY POSSIBLE

Ways to detect
    Cameras,
    Logs
    Lights


Ways to Recover
    Backups
    Dependable logs
    Tracking measures




Principles
    Minimize attack surface
        One door is easier to guard than three doors
        One admin user is easier to guard than three

    Ideally, disable access for everyone to everything unless you authorize it first
        not truly possible
        At least disable everything by default, then start enabling

    Defense in Depth
        lock + door vs lock + door + access control + guard + fence
            measure risk and value to how much in depth it should be
